Smash Burger

Amazing smash burgers made easy

Look no further. This is the last recipe you will ever need
to create the perfect smash burger. Lets dig in!

Ingredients

  • Ground Beef: Aim for an 80/20 blend of lean to fat for optimal flavor
  • Hamburger Buns: Soft, sturdy buns like potato or brioche are ideal
  • Cheese: American cheese slices melt well and are a classic choice
  • Salt and Pepper: Essential for seasoning the patties
  • Toppings (Optional):
    • Lettuce (shredded iceberg or leaf lettuce)
    • Tomato (sliced)
    • Onions (thinly sliced or diced)
    • Pickles (dill or sweet)
    • Ketchup and mustard
    • Burger sauce

Steps

  1. Weigh beef into 2.5oz balls and keep them in the fridge all night.
    Freeze the rest.
  2. Preheat a flat top grill or cast iron skillet to 575°F, check with infrared
    thermometer.
  3. I let it ride for a few seconds, and then, using a very stiff 4" X 3" spatula,
    smash! Don't smash straight down. Press the middle of the ball at a slight
    angle away from you with the thicker side towards the middle. In a slight
    smearing motion, spread the meat pretty hard into the pan. If all goes well,
    and your pan is hot enough, the meat should stick to the pan and not your
    spatula. Working quickly, go around the rest of the patty, smashing the edges
    more than the middle. That is how you get those awesome lacy edges while
    still having a bit of body to the burger.
  4. Season the patty with a generous amount of salt and pepper.
  5. Watch the edges and look for liquid coming up to the top. Once the edges look
    browned on the bottom and cooked through, the center should still have a touch
    of pink in it with lots of liquid rising to the top.
  6. Scrape hard and fairly quickly to get them to release and then flip.
  7. Add cheese and cover with a lid, wait about 30 seconds to steam and melt.
  8. Transfer to prepared buns that have been toasted with butter.
  9. Dress with condiments and toppings of choice!

If I'm making a good bit of them, I keep them on a rack in a sheet pan in the oven
set to 200°F after they're assembled.

And thats all it takes. Get ready to have the burger of a lifetime!
